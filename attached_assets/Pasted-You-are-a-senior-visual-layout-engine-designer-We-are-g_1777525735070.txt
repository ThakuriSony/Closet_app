You are a senior visual layout engine designer.

We are generating Pinterest-style outfit compositions.

Previous attempts failed because items were placed without normalization and visual weight control.

You MUST follow a structured composition system.

---

GOAL:

Create a clean, editorial outfit layout that looks like a fashion flat-lay from Pinterest.

---

STEP 1 — NORMALIZE ALL IMAGES (CRITICAL)

Before layout:

For each item:
- Trim excess transparent/white padding
- Center the clothing item
- Fit into a consistent bounding box

Use category-based boxes:

- Top: square (1:1)
- Bottom: vertical (3:4)
- Shoes: horizontal (4:3)
- Accessories: small square

---

STEP 2 — DEFINE LAYOUT STRUCTURE

Use a 2-column asymmetrical layout:

LEFT (dominant):
- Bottom (anchor, largest)
- Top (above it)

RIGHT (supporting):
- Shoes
- Accessories (grouped)

Outerwear:
- Optional, near top, slightly offset

---

STEP 3 — ANCHOR SYSTEM

- Bottom item is the anchor
- All other items positioned relative to bottom

---

STEP 4 — VISUAL WEIGHT CONTROL

Adjust size dynamically:

- If item is visually heavy → reduce scale
- If item is small → slightly increase

Goal:
Balanced composition, not equal sizes

---

STEP 5 — ALIGNMENT RULES

- Maintain vertical alignment spine
- Keep consistent spacing between items
- Avoid floating randomness

---

STEP 6 — GROUP ACCESSORIES

- Place accessories as a cluster
- Keep them close together
- Treat as one visual unit

---

STEP 7 — NEGATIVE SPACE

- Leave 25–35% empty space
- Do NOT fill container

---

STEP 8 — FINAL POLISH

- Add subtle shadow under items
- Ensure no overlapping chaos
- Maintain clean spacing

---

OUTPUT:

Return structured layout with:

- position
- size
- alignment references

---

ANTI-PATTERNS:

- ❌ raw placement without normalization
- ❌ equal spacing
- ❌ equal sizes

- curated fashion layout
